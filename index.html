About

I'm a predoctoral research fellow at the Policy Lab for Education and Labor Markets, where I work with Prof. Dana Whitfield and Prof. Marcus Lee on causal inference projects related to school choice and early-career labor outcomes. Before this, I completed my BA in Economics and Statistics at Aldridge College, where I wrote my honors thesis on apprenticeship program design.

My research interests sit at the intersection of labor economics, education policy, and applied econometrics — I'm especially drawn to questions where a well-identified empirical design can speak directly to a live policy debate.

// 01 research

Research

Working paper
Apprenticeship Exposure and Early-Career Wage Trajectories
with Dana Whitfield and Marcus Lee

Using administrative wage records linked to a state apprenticeship registry, we estimate the effect of apprenticeship participation on wage growth over the first decade of a worker's career. We exploit variation in program capacity across cohorts to address selection into apprenticeship enrollment, finding that participants experience steeper early wage growth relative to matched non-participants, with effects concentrated among workers without a four-year degree.

In progress
School Choice and Sibling Spillovers: Evidence from a Centralized Lottery
solo-authored

I study whether a sibling's assignment to an oversubscribed school affects younger siblings' later academic outcomes, using lottery-based variation from a centralized enrollment system. Preliminary results suggest modest positive spillovers on attendance and test scores, consistent with shared information or within-family peer effects.

Undergraduate thesis
Designing for Persistence: Completion Rates Across Apprenticeship Program Structures
Aldridge College, advised by Prof. Helen Ostrander

I document substantial heterogeneity in completion rates across apprenticeship program structures and test which design features — wage progression schedules, mentor assignment, and credential stacking — best predict persistence, using program-level data from three state registries.
