About

I'm a Research Assistant in the Department of Social Policy and Intervention at the University of Oxford, where I work on evidence synthesis and economic analysis for adolescent health and development programming, in collaboration with UNICEF and under Gates Foundation–funded projects. I hold an MSc in Economics for Development from Oxford and a joint BSc/BEcon in Economics from the University of Liverpool and Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University.

My work sits at the intersection of development economics, health economics, and policy evaluation — I'm particularly interested in using rigorous quantitative methods (cost-effectiveness modelling, systematic review and meta-analysis, impact evaluation) to inform real investment and policy decisions, especially around child marriage, child labour, education, and adolescent health in low- and middle-income countries.
